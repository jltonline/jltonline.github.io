The Journal of Literary Theory (JLT) sees itself as a forum for different positions in literary theory and is consistently dedicated to systematic and theoretical-historical topics. The journal has been published by De Gruyter since 2007. It is interdisciplinary and open to all approaches to literary theory. In addition to contributions on literary theory in the narrower sense, it includes related fields of research, such as musicology, art history and film studies.

JLT publishes research articles on systematic problems of literary theory, methodology, the structure of theories and on conceptualisation, as well as on individual approaches to literary theory. It also takes up international theoretical debates. Individual case studies – i.e., studies on individual authors, works or problems of literary history – are only considered if they have been analysed for their systematic value, make a contribution to the reconstruction of the history of literary theory, or present an innovative method. Most issues of the journal are devoted to a specific topic; however, free contributions are also welcome.

The Journal of Literary Theory is published twice a year. Calls for articles are published in advance outlining an issue’s main topic. Contributions can be submitted in English or German. All contributions are published together with an abstract of approximately 800 words in English. Submitted manuscripts are reviewed by the international advisory board and external scholars in a peer review process that is anonymous on both sides. Information on setting up and submitting articles for the issues of the JLT can be found in the for authors section.

Occasionally individual journal issues are supervised by guest editors. If you are interested in editing a guest issue, you will also find information on this in the for authors section.
